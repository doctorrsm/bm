What is Babylon the Great?

One of the most enigmatic images of Revelation is the mysterious prositute named Babylon.

The woman is the great city, which reigns over the kings of the earth.

 

  • With whom the kings of the earth committed sexual immorality. Those who dwell in the earth were made drunken with the wine of her sexual immorality.
  • In her was found the blood of prophets and of saints, and of all who have been slain on the earth.
  • For she says in her heart, ‘I sit a queen, and am no widow, and will in no way see mourning.’ Therefore in one day her plagues will come: death, mourning, and famine; and she will be utterly burned with fire; for the Lord God who has judged her is strong.

There are many versions of who this prostitute represents.Without taking any of the opposing sides, we will reveal this mystery to you with the help of the Bible.

1 One of the seven angels who had the seven bowls came and spoke with me, saying, “Come here. Christianity will also break its covenant with God and kill God's faithful witnesses. The prostitute Babylon the Great from Revelation is the mystery of the apostasy of Christianity.

Babylon

The people at that time had one language, and they wanted to worship themselves by making a name for themselves. But God ruined their plans by mixing or dividing their tongues. Therefore Babylon means mixture.

This story is a prophetic image. The book of Daniel compares states to beasts. And Nimrod, the great beastmaster, is an image of the devil trapping men and kings of the earth in his net. (Luke 4:5,6; Revelation 13:1,2).

29 For I know that after my departure, vicious wolves will enter in among you, not sparing the flock. 30 Men will arise from among your own selves, speaking perverse things, to draw away the disciples after them. (Act 20:29,30)

The Two Witnesses Bring the Light of Truth from God to People

In the first century, the Jewish people lived in hope of the coming of the Messiah. First came John the Baptist, who, in the spirit of Elijah the prophet, prepared the hearts of the people for the coming of the Lord. Then came Jesus, who, in the spirit of Moses, led his people out of the slavery of sin and death.

13 For all the prophets and the law prophesied until John. 14 If you are willing to receive it, this is Elijah, who is to come. 15 He who has ears to hear, let him hear. (Matthew 11:13-15)

37 This is the Moses who said to the Israelites: God will raise up for you a prophet like me from among your brothers. Listen to him. (Acts 7:37)

Biblical history seems to spiral as events repeat themselves, only on a larger scale. Christ said:

12 Most certainly I tell you, he who believes in me, the works that I do, he will do also; and he will do greater works than these, because I am going to my Father. (John 14:12).

This means that the ministry of the two witnesses in first century Israel will be repeated in the last days, only on a global scale.

About sixty-five years after the Lord's death, the apostle John recorded about the symbolic Elijah and Moses, the two witnesses of the last days:

3 I will give power to my two witnesses, and they will prophesy one thousand two hundred sixty days, clothed in sackcloth. 6 These have the power to shut up the sky, that it may not rain during the days of their prophecy. They have power over the waters, to turn them into blood, and to strike the earth with every plague, as often as they desire. 7 When they have finished their testimony, the beast that comes up out of the abyss will make war with them, and overcome them, and kill them. 8 Their dead bodies will be in the street of the great city, which spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt, where also their Lord was crucified. Two witnesses

They will do the same work that Elijah, aka John the Baptist, did, preparing people's hearts for the day when the Son of Man is coming in glory. They will also, like Moses and then Christ, turn others to the truth.

They will be a stumbling block to ungodly believers. And therefore, hated by most of the Christian clergy. For this reason they will be led to execution before kings and governors for the Name of Christ (Luk.21:12-17).
